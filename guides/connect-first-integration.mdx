title: "Connect your first integration to VERA"
description: "Step-by-step walkthrough for linking Meta Ads or GoHighLevel CRM as your first data source and unlocking Vera One intelligence and lead scoring."
---

Connecting a platform is the first thing you should do after creating your VERA workspace. The moment an integration is live, VERA starts pulling historical data and Vera One begins building benchmarks for your clients. This guide walks you through the full process from the Setup page to your first synced data.

## Which integration to connect first

Start with **Meta Ads** or **GoHighLevel CRM** — they unlock the most Vera One capabilities. If you only have one available, connect it now and add the second later.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to VERA">
    Go to [app.verahq.ai](https://app.verahq.ai) and sign in to your workspace.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open Setup">
    Click **Setup** in the left sidebar. You'll see a card for each supported platform: Meta Ads, GoHighLevel, Fathom, Calendly, Stripe, and others.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find your integration card">
    Locate the card for the platform you want to connect — **Meta Ads** or **GoHighLevel** are the recommended starting points.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Connect">
    Click the **Connect** button on the card. VERA redirects you to that platform's OAuth authorization screen.

    For GoHighLevel with a Private Integration Token, you'll instead be prompted to paste your token and Location ID directly into the card.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Authorize VERA">
    Complete the authorization on the platform's screen and return to VERA. The integration card updates to show a green connected state with your account name.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for backfill">
    VERA immediately begins a historical backfill — pulling campaigns, contacts, pipeline stages, meetings, and other records that already exist in the platform.

    <Note>
      Backfill can take a few minutes for large accounts. A progress indicator appears on the Setup card while it runs. Vera One intelligence features activate after backfill completes.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify your data">
    Once backfill finishes, your data appears across VERA:
    - Campaign performance shows up on the **Growth** and **Campaigns** pages
    - Contacts and pipeline stages appear on the **Pipeline** page
    - Signals and scored leads surface in the **Intelligence** feed
    - Vera One starts building benchmarks from your historical data
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens after connecting Meta Ads

- All campaigns in your connected ad account appear on the **Campaigns** page
- Leads from Meta lead gen forms begin syncing and are scored by Vera One
- If you have a conversion pixel, it is auto-assigned to matching clients
- CAPI (Conversion API) events begin forwarding automatically for improved attribution

## What happens after connecting GoHighLevel

- Contacts and pipeline stages sync across all your sub-account locations
- Lead attribution begins matching Meta leads to GHL contacts by email and phone
- GHL stage changes (booked, showed, closed) trigger funnel events that feed back to your ad platforms
- The Action Engine can create GHL tasks automatically when Vera One detects hot leads

<Tip>
  Connect both Meta Ads **and** GoHighLevel for the best Vera One intelligence. Cross-platform attribution — matching an ad click to a closed deal in GHL — is where the biggest insights come from.
</Tip>

## Check integration health

After connecting, you can verify that VERA's connection to the platform is live at any time. The Setup page runs a live health check against each platform's API and shows the connected account name.

To programmatically check integration health:

```bash
curl "https://api.verahq.ai/api/v1/integrations/{workspace_id}/health" \
  -H "X-API-Key: YOUR_API_KEY"
```

The response includes a per-platform `healthy` flag and any error messages:

```json
{
  "workspace_id": "ws_abc123",
  "checked_at": "2026-04-19T10:00:00Z",
  "integrations": {
    "meta_ads": { "healthy": true, "error": null, "account": "VERA Agency" },
    "ghl":      { "healthy": true, "error": null, "account": "My Location" }
  }
}
```

## Disconnect an integration

To disconnect, click the connected card on the Setup page and select **Disconnect**. This removes the stored token and stops future syncs. Historical data already imported into VERA is not deleted.
