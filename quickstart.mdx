---
title: "Get started with VERA"
description: "Learn how to create your workspace, connect your first integration, add a client, and run your first AI skill — all in under 15 minutes."
---

This guide walks you through everything you need to go from a blank VERA workspace to running your first AI skill against real client data. Follow the steps in order — each one builds on the last.

<Note>
  API access requires an API key. Once your workspace is created, you can find your key in **Settings → Workspace settings**. See [Authentication](/authentication) for details on how to pass it.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up and create your workspace">
    Go to [app.verahq.ai](https://app.verahq.ai) and sign up. You can sign in with your email address.

    After your first login, VERA creates a workspace automatically and names it after your email address. You can rename it at any time from **Settings → Workspace settings**.

    Your workspace is the top-level container for all your clients, integrations, settings, and AI skill history.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect at least one integration">
    Go to **Setup** and connect an integration. We recommend starting with **Meta Ads** or **GoHighLevel** — they provide the richest data for lead scoring and campaign monitoring.

    VERA uses OAuth for all integrations. Click the **Connect** button next to the platform you want to add and complete the authorization flow. Once connected, VERA begins backfilling historical data automatically — campaigns, contacts, conversations, and meetings are synced in the background so you have accurate benchmarks from day one.

    Supported integrations:
    - **Meta Ads** — campaigns, ad sets, insights, CAPI
    - **GoHighLevel** — contacts, pipelines, conversations
    - **Google Ads** — campaigns and performance data
    - **Fathom** — meeting recordings and summaries
    - **Calendly** — booked events
    - **Stripe** — MRR and subscription history
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your first client">
    Go to **Clients** and click **Add client**. Each client record stores:

    - **Name** — the client or account name displayed across the dashboard
    - **MRR** — monthly recurring revenue, used by Vera One for churn risk and revenue attribution
    - **Platform account IDs** — the ad account IDs and CRM location IDs that belong to this client, so VERA knows which data to pull when running skills or generating reports

    Fill in the name and MRR to start. You can add platform account IDs once you have confirmed which accounts belong to this client.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run your first AI skill">
    Go to **Skills** and select a client. Choose a skill to run — good starting points are:

    - **`meta-ads-analysis`** — reviews campaign performance, identifies underperforming ad sets, and returns optimization recommendations
    - **`lead-scorer`** — scores all contacts in your connected CRM across fit, engagement, timing, and intent

    Click **Run skill**. VERA fetches the latest data for that client, builds a prompt using your connected platform data, and passes it to the AI model. Skills typically complete in 10–30 seconds depending on data volume.

    You can also trigger skills from the client detail page by clicking **Run AI skill** next to the client name.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review results in the Intelligence feed">
    Go to **Intelligence** to see the output. The Intelligence feed is where Vera One surfaces everything it has found: skill results, lead score changes, campaign alerts, attribution events, and churn risk signals.

    Each entry shows the client, the signal type, a timestamp, and the AI-generated output or recommendation. From the feed you can acknowledge alerts, drill into skill results, or trigger follow-up actions directly.
  </Step>
</Steps>
